UX Case Study: Porrada

Empowering Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) Practitioners at Every Level

Project Overview

  • Timeline: May 2025 — Present
  • Author: Sam Sexton

Porrada is a web application created to support Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) athletes - from white belts to black belts - by offering a personalized digital training partner. With features like technique tracking, training logs, instructional libraries, and community-based feedback, Porrada aims to improve progression, structure learning, and foster a strong sense of community among practitioners.

Problem

While BJJ academies offer rich, hands-on instruction, many practitioners struggle with:

  • Retaining techniques post-class
  • Tracking personal progress over time
  • Setting and achieving training goals
  • Finding tailored guidance for their level or style
  • Connecting with other trainees to share insights or ask for help

Existing solutions (YouTube videos, spreadsheets, notebooks, scattered forums) were fragmented, inconsistent, and lacked personalization.

By streamlining training management, Porrada seeks to empower BJJ practitioners to train more deliberately, track their evolution, and ultimately enhance their performance on the mat.

Goal

Design a centralized, intuitive, and engaging platform that:

  • Helps BJJ practitioners capture and review techniques
  • Tracks training frequency, goals, and milestones
  • Enables structured reflection on rolling (sparring) sessions
  • Connects users for peer feedback and community growth
